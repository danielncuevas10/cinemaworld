Top 10 Best Sci-Fi Films

We are counting down the best Sci-Fi films, from very good to perfect, ENJOY!

10_ Metropolis

Directed by: Fritz Lang, 1927.

If there could be a movie that has to be named the mother of all science fiction genre, "Metropolis" must have this name, the story of this film tells us about the lower class living hidden under the city, and the rich people living in the best way possible in the surface, this is a must watch for every sci-fi lover.

Directed by: Alfonso Cuarón, 2006.

One of the most interesting plots ever, it just starts in a terrific way, showing us a world that can not have any more babys, where they all know that they are doomed forever, "Children ok men" is perfecly directed by Alfonso Cuarón, but it has so many sequences that lead us to say that this is a masterpiece with a perfect cinematography, thanks to Emmanuel Lubezki.

8_ Alien

Directed by: Ridley Scott, 1979.

Thriller, drama, suspense, action, space, discovery and a crew fighting for their lifes! Ridley Scott reinvented sci-fi genre and did probably his best work with the amazing experience that we all have lived while watching "Alien", we must say that "Aliens" is another amazing film.

In an era where Artificial Intelligence is growing and where many people is afraid of waht culd happen, this film came to deliver us a perfectely well written script with astonishing CGI and that showed us many important things, this will test you in what is real and what we really are.

6_ The Planet of the Apes

Directed by: Franklin J. Schaffner, 1968.

A cult classic that has to be named, possibly most of us have watched the end of the movie without knowing what movie is about, but based on the book written by Pierre Boulle, it shows us a world that is not dominated by humans, but by apes, eventhough the film has many differences from the book, it really has it essence and importance.

5_ Dune Part 1

Directed by: Denis Villeneuve, 2021.

Another adaptation here, let's not forget that this epic book written by Frank Herbert was already adapted in 1984, eventhough is not that good, it delivered some good sequences, but in 2021, Denis Villenueve exceeded all expectations, delivering us an outstanding cinema experience with asnonishing cinematography and with Hans Zimmer delivering an amazing soundtrack. This story seemed doomed and impossible to adapt, but this is definitely what we were hoping, even more.

Directed by: Robert Zemeckis, 1985.

2_ Blade Runner

Directed by: Ridley Scott, 1982.

A future where "replicants" are so similar to humans that we can not differentiate between us? This premise is really interesting, and even more when a detective is trying to eliminate the last of them, but the issue comes when he is not sure about who is who or what is what, even thinking if he could be one of them.

1_ 2001: A Space Odyssey

Directed by: Stanley Kubrick, 1968.

The epic of epics, there is a before and after in the science fiction genre after this film came out, Stanley Kubrick deliverd his best film and definitely the best film in sci-fi where many movis had taken an inspiration, from AI to time travel, to god itself, this film has been so investigated and brought us to so many debates about many things, that it is impossible not to have watched some sequences from it, taking the limits even further.
